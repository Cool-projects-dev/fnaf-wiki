FNAF 1

Release Date: August 8, 2014

Developer: Scott Cawthon

Engine: Clickteam Fusion 2.5

Genre: Point-and-click survival horror

Platform(s): PC, Mobile, Console (later ports)

🧠 Overview

Five Nights at Freddy’s is the first game in the popular indie horror franchise. Players take the role of Mike Schmidt, a night security guard at Freddy Fazbear’s Pizza, a family restaurant with animatronic characters that become dangerously active after hours.

The game is set in a single room—the security office—where players must survive five nights (and more) using limited resources to monitor and defend against hostile animatronics.

🎮 Gameplay

Players must monitor a network of security cameras to track animatronics and prevent them from entering the office.

Defensive tools include:

  • Door controls (left and right)
  • Hallway lights
  • Security cameras

    • All tools drain limited power, which resets each night. If power runs out, the office goes dark and Freddy Fazbear will attack.

    Objectives:

  • Survive from 12:00 AM to 6:00 AM each night
  • Avoid being caught by any animatronic
  • Manage power usage effectively

    • 🤖 Animatronics

    Name Description Route
    Freddy Fazbear The main mascot. Attacks later in the game, often silently. Right side
    Bonnie the Bunny Very active; appears early. Left side
    Chica the Chicken Often seen holding a cupcake. Right side
    Foxy the Pirate Fox Hides in Pirate Cove; sprints toward the office. Left side
    Golden Freddy Easter egg; causes hallucinations or crashes. Appears randomly

    🧩 Nights & Custom Mode

  • Night 1–5: Core progression
  • Night 6: Extra hard mode unlocked after Night 5
  • Night 7 (Custom Night): Allows players to set AI difficulty (0–20) for each animatronic

    • Notable Challenge:

    "4/20 Mode" – All animatronics set to 20. Considered the ultimate FNaF 1 challenge.

    🔊 Phone Guy

    Each night begins with a voicemail from the previous night guard, offering tips, lore, and warnings. On Night 4, he is seemingly attacked and killed on the call, increasing tension.

    🔒 Lore Summary

    While FNaF 1 doesn’t explain everything directly, it hints at a darker story:

  • Animatronics are believed to be possessed by the spirits of murdered children
  • A man later known as the Purple Guy is responsible
  • The infamous “Bite of ’87” is mentioned, involving severe injury caused by an animatronic
  • Missing children incident posters can be spotted in the game

    • 🔍 Trivia

  • The game was inspired by criticism of Scott Cawthon’s previous games.
  • Golden Freddy was discovered by accident by players.
  • FNaF 1 gained massive popularity due to YouTubers like Markiplier.
  • It became the start of a franchise with deep lore and a dedicated fanbase.
